# Development and validation of the AI attitude scale (AIAS-4)

## Main Thesis

The AI Attitude Scale (AIAS-4) offers a valuable tool for researchers and practitioners to assess general attitudes toward Artificial Intelligence technology. The scale is designed to capture attitudes focusing on perceived utility and potential impact of AI on society and humanity.

## Key Findings

### Scale Development
- Initial 5-item scale refined to 4-item version after EFA revealed Factor 2 had insufficient internal consistency
- Final AIAS-4 is a unidimensional scale measuring positive attitudes toward AI
- Scale items:
 1. "I believe that AI will improve my life"
 2. "I believe that AI will improve my work"
 3. "I think I will use AI technology in the future"
 4. [Fourth item related to AI benefits]

### Psychometric Properties
- **Internal consistency:** Cronbach's α = 0.892, McDonald's ω = 0.892 (Study 1)
- **Model fit (CFA):** CFI = 0.999, TLI = 0.998, RMSEA = 0.0285
- Strong factor loadings (0.757 - 0.941)
- Good convergent validity with MTUAS (r = 0.484 - 0.535)

### Demographic Predictors
- Gender emerged as the only statistically significant predictor
- Female participants showed lower AIAS scores compared to male participants
- Age and education were not significant predictors

## Methodology

**Design:** Two-study validation (EFA followed by CFA with independent sample)
**Sample:**
- Study 1: UK participants (convenience sample)
- Study 2: 300 USA participants
**Methods:**
- Exploratory Factor Analysis (EFA) with oblimin rotation
- Confirmatory Factor Analysis (CFA)
- Maximum likelihood extraction
- Bartlett's test of sphericity: χ²(10) = 612, p < 0.001

## Strengths

- Short scale (4 items) - easy to administer alongside other measures
- Good psychometric properties confirmed across two samples
- Open access scale available on Zenodo (CC BY 4.0)
- Theoretically grounded in TAM (Technology Acceptance Model)
- Clear unidimensional factor structure

## Limitations

- **Sample generalizability:** Convenience samples from UK and USA only
- **Cultural validity:** May not generalize to populations with different demographic or cultural backgrounds
- **Unidimensional focus:** Only measures positive attitudes toward AI, does not capture negative attitudes or concerns
- **Domain-general:** Not specific to any AI application domain (e.g., mental health)
- **Factor 2 issues:** Original negative/risk perception items had to be dropped due to poor reliability
